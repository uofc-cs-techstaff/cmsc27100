Here, we'll prove the equivalence of the contrapositive.

Let $p$ and $q$ be propositions. Prove that if $p \rightarrow q$ is true, then $\neg q \rightarrow \neg p$.

Hidden.

 Suppose $p \rightarrow q$ is true. Assume $\neg q$ is true. Assume $p$ is true. Since $p$ is true, we can conclude $q$ is true. But this is a contradiction. Therefore, our assumption that $p$ was true was incorrect. Therefore, $\neg p$. Therefore, $\neg q \rightarrow \neg p$.
