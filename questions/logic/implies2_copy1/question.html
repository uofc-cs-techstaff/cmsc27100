Let $p$, $q$, and $r$ be propositions. Prove that if $p \rightarrow q$ and $q \rightarrow r$ are true, then $p \rightarrow r$.

Suppose $p \rightarrow q$ is true. Suppose $q \rightarrow r$ is true. Assume $p$ is true. Since $p$ is true, from $p \rightarrow q$, we can conclude $q$ is true. Since $q$ is true, from $q \rightarrow r$, we can conclude $r$ is true. Therefore, we can conclude $p \rightarrow r$.
