Let $p$, $q$, and $r$ be propositions. Prove that if $r \rightarrow p \land q$ and $\neg q$, then $\neg r$.

Suppose $r \rightarrow p \land q$ is true. Assume $r$ is true. Suppose $\neg q$ is true. Since $r$ is true and $r \rightarrow p \land q$ is true, we can conclude that $p \land q$ is true. Since $p \land q$ is true we know $q$ is true. But we have that $q$ is true and $\neg q$ is true, which is a contradiction. Therefore, our assumption was incorrect and $\neg r$ must be true.
