Prove that for any graph $G$, the number of vertices of odd degree in $G$ is even.

Hidden.

 Let $V_{odd}, V_{even} \subseteq V$ be the sets of odd and even degree vertices of $G$, respectively. By the Handshaking lemma, \[2|E| = \sum_{v \in V_{odd}} \deg(v) + \sum_{v \in V_{even}} \deg(v).\] By definition, $\deg(v)$ is even for all $v \in V_{even}$. Therefore, $\sum\limits_{v \in V_{even}} \deg(v)$ is even. Since $\sum\limits_{v \in V_{even}} \deg(v)$ is even and $2|E|$ is even, $\sum\limits_{v \in V_{odd}} \deg(v)$ is even. But $\deg(v)$ is odd for all $v \in V_{odd}$, so $|V_1|$ must be even.
