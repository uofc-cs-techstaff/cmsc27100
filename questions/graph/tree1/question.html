Let $T$ be a tree with more than one vertex. Then $T$ contains at least two leaves.

Hidden.

 Consider a longest path in $T$. Let $u$ be a vertex at one end of this path. Suppose there is an edge between $u$ and a vertex $w$ not on the path. Then we can make an even longer path, to $w$, contradicting maximality of our path. Therefore, the edge $(u,w)$ cannot exist. Since there is more than one vertex in $T$, our path must contain an edge $(u,v)$. The edge $(u,v)$ is the last edge on our path. Suppose there is an edge between $u$ and a vertex $x \neq v$ on the path. Then there is a cycle: take the edge from $x$ to $u$, then from $u$ to $v$, then from $v$ to $x$. Therefore, the edge $(u,x)$ cannot exist. Therefore, $v$ is the only vertex adjacent to $u$. Therefore, $\deg(u) = 1$. We apply a similar argument to the vertex on the other end of the path to get our second vertex of degree 1.
