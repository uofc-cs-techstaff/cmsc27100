Prove that every composite number $n \geq 2$ has a prime factor $p \leq \sqrt n$.

Hidden.

 Let $n$ be an arbitrary composite integer and $n \geq 2$. Then $n$ can be written as a product of two integers $n = a \cdot b$. Without loss of generality, $a \leq b$. Suppose that $a \gt \sqrt n$. Then $b \gt \sqrt n$, since $b \geq a$. Then $a \cdot b \gt \sqrt n \cdot \sqrt n = n$. This is a contradiction. Therefore, $a \leq \sqrt n$. By the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, $a$ has a prime factor, say $p$. Since $p \mid a$ and $a \mid n$, we have $p \mid n$. Therefore $p$ is a prime and a factor of $n$. Since $p \mid a$ and $a \leq \sqrt n$, we have $p \leq a \leq \sqrt n$. Therefore $p \leq \sqrt n$ and $p$ is a prime factor of $n$.
