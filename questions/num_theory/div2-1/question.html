Prove for all integers $a,b,c$ that if $a \mid b$, then $a \mid b \cdot c$.

Hidden.

 Let $a,b,c$ be arbitrary integers. Assume that $a \mid b$. There exists an integer $n$ such that $a \cdot n = b$ by definition of divisibility. Consider the integer $c \cdot b$. We have $c \cdot b = c \cdot (a \cdot n) = a \cdot (c \cdot n)$. Since $c \cdot n$ is an integer, we have $a \mid b \cdot c$ by definition of divisibility.
