Prove that if $a \mid b$ and $b \mid a$, then $a = \pm b$.

Hidden.

 Let $a,b$ be arbitrary integers. Assume that $a \mid b$ and $b \mid a$. There exists an integer $m$ such that $a \cdot m = b$ by definition of divisibility. There exists an integer $n$ such that $b \cdot n = a$ by definition of divisibility. We have $b = b \cdot m \cdot n$. Therefore, $m \cdot n = 1$. The only integers $m$ and $n$ such that $m \cdot n = 1$ are $m = n = 1$ or $m = n = -1$. If $m = n = 1$, then $a = b$. If $m = n = -1$, then $a = -b$. Therefore $a = \pm b$.
