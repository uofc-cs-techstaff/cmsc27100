Let $A$ be an event and let $B$ be a trivial event. Prove that $A$ and $B$ are independent..

Hidden.

 Since $B$ is trivial, $B = \emptyset$ or $B = \Omega$. Suppose $B = \emptyset$. Then $\Pr(B) = 0$. Then $A \cap B = \emptyset$. Therefore $\Pr(A \cap B) = 0$. Thus, $\Pr(A) \Pr(B) = \Pr(A) \cdot 0 = 0$. Since $\Pr(A)\Pr(B) = 0 = \Pr(A \cap B)$, $A$ and $B$ are independent. Suppose $B = \Omega$. Then $\Pr(B) = 1$. Then $A \cap B = A$. Therefore $\Pr(A \cap B) = \Pr(A)$. Thus, $\Pr(A) \Pr(B) = P(A) \cdot 1 = \Pr(A)$. Since $\Pr(A)\Pr(B) = \Pr(A) = \Pr(A \cap B)$, $A$ and $B$ are independent. Therefore, $A$ and $B$ are independent.
