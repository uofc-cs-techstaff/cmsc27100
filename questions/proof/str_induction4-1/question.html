Denote the Fibonacci numbers by $\{f_n\}_{n \geq 0}$.

Prove that $f_{n+m} = f_m f_{n+1} + f_{m-1} f_n$ for all $n \geq 0$ and $m \geq 1$.

Therefore, the base case holds. Inductive case. Let $k \in \mathbb N$. Assume that $f_{n+i} = f_i f_{n+1} + f_{i-1} f_n$ holds for all $i$ with $1 \leq i \leq k$. Consider $m = k+1$. By our inductive hypothesis, $f_{n+(m-1)} = f_{m-1} f_n + f_{n+1} f_{m-2}$ and $f_{n+(m-2)} = f_{m-2} f_n + f_{n+1} f_{m-3}$. Therefore, the inductive case holds. Therefore, we have shown that $f_{n+m} = f_m f_{n+1} + f_{m-1} f_n$ by induction.
