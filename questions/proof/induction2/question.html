Let $k \in \mathbb N$. Assume that $(1+x)^k \geq 1 + kx$. Consider $n = k+1$. Then $(1+x)^{k+1} = (1+x)^k \cdot (1+x)$. By our inductive hypothesis, $(1+x)^k \cdot (1+x) \geq (1+kx)(1+x)$. Then $(1+kx)(1+x) = 1+kx+x+kx^2 = 1 + (k+1)x + kx^2$. Since $x \geq 0$ and $k \in \mathbb N$, we have $kx^2 \geq 0$. Therefore $1 + (k+1)x + kx^2 \geq 1 + (k+1)x$. Therefore, the inductive case holds. Therefore, $(1+x)^n \geq 1 + nx$ holds for all $n \in \mathbb N$ and $x \geq 0$. We will prove this by induction on $x$.
